## The rules

Before posting anything promotional:
1. Spend 7 days being genuinely helpful in the group
2. Answer 5-10 questions from other photographers
3. Like and comment on others' work
4. Build recognition as a helpful member

Then post. Not before.

## Post 2: The Pain Point Hook

Post after you've been in the group a while. Opens conversation.

```
How much time do you all spend on QC before delivery?

I was averaging 30 minutes per property manually checking verticals, color, composition. On a busy week that was 15-20 hours just on review.

Curious what others do:
1. Do it yourself
2. Outsourced editor handles QC
3. Skip QC entirely (hope for the best)
4. Something else?

Dropping a comment with my current workflow if anyone's interested.
```

Then in the comments:
```
My current workflow: shoot, cull in Photo Mechanic, rough edit in Lightroom, THEN I run everything through PhotoQC which catches what I missed. Saves me 20+ hrs/week. Link in bio if you want to try.
```

## Comment Engagement Templates

When someone else posts about QC/editing pain, DON'T jump to pitch. Engage:

```
Been there. The verticals thing alone used to eat 20 min per property for me.

[Then give real advice, no pitch]
```

Later if they ask "how do you handle it now?":

```
Honestly automated it. I built a QC tool for my own company that does the vertical check, color, and composition. Happy to share more if you want, just don't want to spam the group.
```

Let them ask. Never volunteer the pitch in comments.

---

## What NOT to Post

- "Check out my new tool!" (feels like spam)
- "Anyone want to buy my QC software?" (feels like a sales pitch)
- Screenshots of the dashboard without context
- Links without explanation
- Anything that feels like a marketing agency wrote it

## What WORKS

- Founder-to-founder language
- Specific pain points others nod their heads to
- Real numbers (time saved, issues found)
- Screenshots of actual QC catches
- Offering genuine help before asking for anything

---

## Cadence

Week 1: Be helpful. No posts.
Week 2: Post 1 (founder story).
Week 3: Post 2 (pain point hook).
Week 4: Respond to comments. No new posts.
Week 5: Post 3 (before/after showcase) OR Post 4 (direct ask) depending on early traction.
Week 6+: Post 5 (case study) once you have one.

Do this across 5-10 groups simultaneously but STAGGER the posts. Don't post the same thing in all 10 on the same day. Admins will ban you.
