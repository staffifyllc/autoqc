# PhotoQC Demo Video Script

A 90-second video for the homepage and cold outreach.

## Equipment

- Screen recorder: QuickTime (Mac) or Loom
- Microphone: your phone or built-in laptop mic is fine
- Software: PhotoQC running on localhost:3000 or production
- Pre-loaded: a demo agency with 1 style profile and 20 reference photos

## Total runtime: 90 seconds

---

## Script

### Hook (0-10 seconds)
**Screen:** Show a stack of RAW photos opening in Lightroom. Scroll through showing crooked walls, mixed color, blown windows.

**Voiceover:**
"Real estate photographers spend hours every week manually QC'ing these issues. Verticals, color, composition. On every shoot."

### Problem (10-25 seconds)
**Screen:** Show a clock/timer running. Cursor slowly clicking through each photo in Lightroom's vertical correction tool.

**Voiceover:**
"30 minutes per property, sometimes more. Nights and weekends. And you still miss stuff. Toilet lid up. Reflection in a mirror. Color shift between rooms."

### Solution (25-45 seconds)
**Screen:** PhotoQC dashboard, drag-and-drop a property upload.

**Voiceover:**
"PhotoQC runs every QC check automatically. Upload a shoot..."

**Screen:** Property uploading, progress bar.

**Voiceover:**
"...and in under a minute, you get a full QC report."

### Results (45-70 seconds)
**Screen:** Property detail page showing photo grid with QC scores, status badges (PASSED, FIXED, FLAGGED).

**Voiceover:**
"Verticals within one degree, color consistency across the set, exposure balance for window views. Auto-corrects what it can. Flags the rest for quick review."

**Screen:** Click into a photo, show the before/after slider moving back and forth.

**Voiceover:**
"Watch this. Crooked walls. Corrected. Green fluorescent cast. Removed. Tilted horizon. Leveled."

### Differentiator (70-80 seconds)
**Screen:** Click to Style Profiles page, showing uploaded reference photos.

**Voiceover:**
"And it's not a generic check. Upload your reference photos once, PhotoQC learns your style. Every check runs against YOUR standard."

### Close (80-90 seconds)
**Screen:** Back to dashboard. Click "Push to Aryeo" button. Show confirmation.

**Voiceover:**
"Approved photos push straight to Aryeo, HDPhotoHub, Spiro, or Tonomo. First three properties free. Ten dollars per property after that."

**Screen:** End card with URL and "Try the demo" CTA.

**Voiceover:**
"Stop doing QC manually. Visit photoqc.com slash demo."

## Shot List

Before recording, have these ready on screen:

1. Lightroom with 20 real estate photos loaded (horizontal crooked walls visible)
2. PhotoQC dashboard with a test agency logged in
3. A pre-uploaded property with QC run complete (mix of PASSED/FIXED/FLAGGED)
4. Browser tab on Style Profiles page with reference photos
5. End card image (create in Canva, your logo + URL)

## Recording tips

- Record in 1080p minimum (4K better for social)
- Keep cursor movements smooth and deliberate
- Pause 1-2 sec on important UI elements so viewers can read
- Keep total under 90 sec - attention spans are brutal
- Add subtle background music (royalty-free from YouTube Audio Library)

## Where to use

- Landing page hero (auto-play muted with subtitles)
- YouTube (full audio + subtitles)
- Instagram Reels (vertical 9:16 recut, 60 sec max)
- TikTok (same as IG)
- Cold email (Loom link)
- Facebook group posts

## Variations

### 30-second cut (for social)
Cut the problem section, go straight from hook to solution. End with CTA.

### 3-minute deep dive (for YouTube)
Expand every section. Show the Style Profile learning. Show Client Profile overrides. Show every auto-fix happening in detail.

### 15-second ad (for paid)
Just show: Before photo -> PhotoQC logo swoosh -> After photo -> "photoqc.com"

---

## First version - record it yourself in 10 minutes

Don't overthink the first version. Open QuickTime, hit Cmd+Shift+5, record your screen talking through the script. Ship it. Iterate based on feedback.

If you hate your voice on camera, use ElevenLabs or similar AI voice for v1, re-record with your voice for v2 after you see what works.
